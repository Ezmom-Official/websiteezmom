5 Ways You Can Add A Bit Of Extra Vegetables To Your Kids' Diet!

It can be a drag getting your kid to eat their vegetables. From one side, you know that they need vegetables to grow strong and healthy, but the daily fight that comes with getting them to eat it can be exhausting. It feels like a never-ending riddle every time. Although it is important to keep exposing your kids to the greens so they will open up to eating them normally, here we have also put together a list that can help you if you want them to eat a few extra greens!

  • Add ‘em to a smoothie! If your kiddo loves sweet, fruity smoothies, chances are they’ll still love them as much if you add a little sneaky vegetable in! Vegetable leaves are always easy to add in, but there really is no limit. Adding avocado can make your smoothie rich and soft, while something like carrots could keep the smoothie very sweet. Beetroot is always something fun to add in, as that can paint the drink bright pink!
  • Put them in muffins! Of course, it is no secret that carrots work good in baked goods -take one look at a delicious carrot cake and you’ll know -but other vegetables, like zucchini, also work great! Your little one might not even know that the vegetable is in the muffin!
  • It’s summer and you can add it to something you bake on the grill and put it between bread... hamburgers of course! Cut a few vegetables up in small pieces and add them to the meat (or meat substitute), and you bet your kids will be asking for seconds!
  • After dinner you might want a little dessert to finish the meal off, and even there you can sneak in a bit of your greens! A vegan chocolate mousse with avocado in it might be just the way to go!
  • Vegetables can be added in so many places -even in your kid’s favorite foods! By adding blended cauliflower, zucchini and onion to the sauce, your kids will still get all the joy from the food while also getting the vegetables!

These are just a few examples, but make sure to experiment and figure out what works best for you and your kids!
